# Credits

HamClock would not be possible without the following resources:

* [Space Weather Prediction Center](https://www.swpc.noaa.gov/) for several data sets:
    * [Sunspot number](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/json/solar-cycle/swpc_observed_ssn.json)
    * [Solar flux monthly](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/text/daily-solar-indices.txt) and [current](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/text/wwv.txt)
    * [Aurora](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/json/ovation_aurora_latest.json)
    * [Scales](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/products/noaa-scales.json)
    * [DRAP](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/text/drap_global_frequencies.txt)
    * [X-Ray](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/json/goes/primary/xrays-3-day.json)
    * [Solar wind](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/products/solar-wind/plasma-3-day.json)
    * [K index](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/text/daily-geomagnetic-indices.txt) and [K and SFI forecast](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/text/3-day-solar-geomag-predictions.txt)
    * [Bz and Bt](https://services.swpc.noaa.gov/text/ace-magnetometer.txt)
* Jari Perkimki, Gregory Hand and Jim Watson for generous [VOACAP](https://www.voacap.com/) support.
* DX cluster [operators](https://www.ng3k.com/misc/cluster.html) and spotters the world over.
* [SOTA](https://www.sota.org.uk/), [POTA](https://pota.app/) and [WWFF](https://wwff.co/) users and their respective data base maintainers.
* Bruce Horn, WA7BNM, for the [8 day context calendar](https://www.contestcalendar.com/weeklycont.php).
* [Celestrak](https://www.celestrak.com/) for timely satellite elements.
* NASA for [SDO](https://sdo.gsfc.nasa.gov/) and [STEREO](https://stereo.gsfc.nasa.gov/) images.
* [National Research Council Canada](https://nrc.canada.ca/en) for 70 years of continuous [10.7 cm solar flux data](https://www.spaceweather.gc.ca/forecast-prevision/solar-solaire/solarflux/sx-en.php) and Dr. Ken Tapping for his gracious support to the amateur radio community.
* [Open Weather Map](https://openweathermap.org/) for near-real-time world-wide weather data.
* Philip Gladstone, N1DQ, for assistance and live data from his web site [PSKReport.info](http://pskreporter.info/).
* Pete Smith, N4ZR, and Felipe Ceglia, PY1NB, for their innovation and tireless work behind the scenes at the [Reverse Beacon Network](https://reversebeacon.net/).
* Arne for assistance and live data from his site [wspr.live](https://wspr.live/).
* NOAA for the [World Magnetic Model](https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/products/world-magnetic-model) and their [Science on a Sphere](https://sos.noaa.gov/) real-time cloud maps.
* Davidson Francis for his [Websocket server](https://github.com/Theldus/wsServer).
* Andrew D. Rodland, KC2G, for providing ready access to the data behind his [ionosonde map](https://prop.kc2g.com/) which in turn are provided by [these organizations](https://prop.kc2g.com/acknowledgments).
* Sean Barrett for compact and easy-to-use image format writer [here](https://github.com/nothings/stb/blob/master/stb_image_write.h).
* Jim Reisert, AD1C, for maintaining the [cty country file](https://www.country-files.com/).
* ITU and CQ zones based on data from [zone-check.eu](https://zone-check.eu/).
* Mario, DL4MFM, for his support and assistance with data from [WWFF](https://wwff.co/).
* DX expedition data from [NG3K](https://www.ng3k.com/Misc/adxo.html) and [DXNews](https://dxnews.com/dxpeditions)
* World Data Center for Geomagnetism, Kyoto University, for [DST data](https://wdc.kugi.kyoto-u.ac.jp/wdc/Sec3.html).
* And special Shoutouts to [Adafruit](https://www.adafruit.com/about) and [Raspberry Pi Foundation](https://www.raspberrypi.org/about) for great products and support.
